## 1. Executive Summary

Adcraft Academy is an advanced Amazon PPC skills training platform that bridges Filipino virtual assistants from foundational PPC knowledge into high-paying agency and direct-client roles. Unlike theory-heavy courses, Adcraft combines structured online classes with interactive hands-on tools (Campaign Builder, Bid Elevator, Search Term Triage) to produce job-ready Amazon advertising specialists.

**Key Points:**
- Problem: Filipino VAs are stuck in low-paying general VA roles because they lack structured advanced PPC training and a clear path to agency employment.
- Solution: A platform combining live online classes, interactive practice tools, badge-based gamification, and job placement outcomes to create a complete VA-to-specialist pipeline.
- Target Users: PPC Companion graduates, experienced PPC specialists, agency-bound Filipino VAs.
- Timeline: Immediate — ASAP launch.

## 2. Problem Statement

### The Problem

Filipino virtual assistants who complete foundational PPC training hit a ceiling. They can manage basic campaigns but lack the advanced skills (bid optimization, search term triage, portfolio strategy, agency-level reporting) required to command ₱80k+/month roles. Existing advanced training is either too expensive, too theoretical, or not tailored to the Philippine VA market.

### Who Experiences This Problem

**Primary Users:**
- **PPC Companion graduates** who want the next level — they already know fundamentals and need advanced实战 skills.
- **Experienced PPC specialists** (6–24 months) who want structured certification and agency placement.
- **Career-switching VAs** with 2+ years general VA experience moving into the PPC niche.

**Secondary Users:**
- **Small agencies** needing a pipeline of trained Amazon Ads specialists.
- **Freelancers** wanting to raise their rates from ₱15k–₱30k/month to ₱60k–₱80k+/month.

### Current Situation

**How Users Currently Handle This:**
- Online self-study via YouTube, Udemy, and scattered blog posts.
- Trial-and-error on Upwork/Freelancer — costly mistakes on live client accounts.
- Expensive Western courses ($500–$2,000) not tailored to the Philippine market context.

**Pain Points:**
- No structured path from "knows PPC basics" to "agency-ready specialist."
- Employers want proof of practical skills, not just certificates.
- Existing tools are theoretical — no sandbox environment to practice campaign building, bid adjustments, or keyword triage.
- Hard to demonstrate competence without a portfolio of real campaign work.

### Impact & Urgency

**Impact if Unsolved:** Filipino VAs remain stuck in low-paying generalist roles. The opportunity to capture the growing Amazon advertising market shifts to other countries.

**Why Now:** Amazon advertising spend continues to grow. Agencies are hungry for trained specialists. PPC Companion's graduate base creates a ready audience. No one is delivering structured advanced training for the Philippine VA market at this price point.

**Frequency:** Continuous learning cycle — students engage weekly through live classes and daily through interactive tools.

## 4. Proposed Solution

### Solution Overview

Adcraft Academy is a Next.js web application that delivers advanced Amazon PPC training through a combination of:

1. **Live Online Classes** — Instructor-led sessions covering advanced strategies, real campaign reviews, and Q&A.
2. **Interactive Practice Tools** — Sandbox environments where students build campaigns, optimize bids, and triage search terms without risk.
3. **Gamified Curriculum** — Structured modules with badges, progress tracking, and quizzes.
4. **Certification & Placement Pipeline** — Graduates earn certificates and get connected with partner agencies.

### Key Capabilities

1. **Advanced Course Curriculum**
   - Description: Structured modules covering portfolio bidding, placement optimization, competitive analysis, agency reporting, and client management.
   - User Value: Transforms theoretical knowledge into agency-ready skills.

2. **Campaign Builder**
   - Description: Interactive tool to practice campaign structures (SP, SB, SD) with real scenarios.
   - User Value: Build muscle memory for campaign architecture without spending real ad budget.

3. **Bid Elevator**
   - Description: Bid optimization simulation tool — practice placement adjustments, portfolio bidding, and bid-by-adjustment strategies.
   - User Value: Master the art of bid management before touching client accounts.

4. **Search Term Triage**
   - Description: Keyword analysis and segmentation tool — classify search terms, identify negatives, find opportunities.
   - User Value: Develop the analytical eye that separates junior from senior optimizers.

5. **Progress & Certification**
   - Description: Badges, module completion tracking, and printable certificates.
   - User Value: Tangible proof of skills for job applications and agency proposals.

### What Makes This Different

**Unique Value Proposition:**
Ryan Roland Dabao — 13 years as a VA, 8 years as an Amazon PPC specialist, ₱50M+ ad spend managed. This is not a course built by a textbook trainer. It is built by someone who has sat in the same chair as every student and climbed to the top. Students learn from real experience, not theory.

### Minimum Viable Solution

**Core Features for MVP:**
1. Course curriculum with live class scheduling and module tracking
2. Campaign Builder interactive tool (reuse components from PPC Companion)
3. Badges and progress tracking
4. Certificate generation on completion
5. Integration with PPC Companion (shared auth or SSO)

**Deferred to Later:**
- Advanced AI-driven practice scenarios (requires budget)
- Agency-direct application portal
- Community forum

## 6. Market & Competition

### Market Context

**Market Size:** Thousands of Filipino VAs actively seeking PPC specialization. The Amazon advertising ecosystem continues to grow 15–20% year over year.

**Market Trends:**
- Agencies increasingly prefer certified specialists over general VAs.
- Remote work has expanded the addressable market for Filipino talent.
- Self-paced micro-learning is overtaking traditional long-form courses.

**Target Market Segment:** Filipino VAs with 1+ year of experience looking to specialize in Amazon PPC.

### Competitive Landscape

#### Competitor 1: Western PPC Courses (e.g., Ad Badger, PPC Mastery)
- **Strengths:** Established brands, comprehensive content, recognized certifications.
- **Weaknesses:** $500–$2,000 price point, not tailored to Philippine market, no interactive tools.
- **Pricing:** $500–$2,000 per course.
- **Market Position:** Premium, inaccessible to most Filipino VAs.

#### Competitor 2: YouTube / Free Content
- **Strengths:** Free, accessible, wide variety.
- **Weaknesses:** No structure, no certification, no hands-on practice, no community.
- **Pricing:** Free (but costs time).
- **Market Position:** Low barrier, low accountability.

#### Competitor 3: Local PPC Training Groups
- **Strengths:** Tagalog-friendly, community-based.
- **Weaknesses:** Inconsistent quality, no interactive tools, limited advanced content.
- **Pricing:** ₱1,000–₱5,000 per course.
- **Market Position:** Entry-level, can't command premium pricing.

### Competitive Advantages

**Our Advantages:**
- Ryan's 13 years VA + 8 years PPC authority — unmatched credibility in the Philippine market.
- Interactive tools (Campaign Builder, Bid Elevator, Search Term Triage) — learn by doing, not just watching.
- Integrated pipeline: PPC Companion → Adcraft Academy → Agency placement.
- Price-accessible for the Philippine market while maintaining premium positioning.
- Filipino-cultural context — understands the specific challenges and opportunities of the local market.

**Gaps We Need to Close:**
- Brand awareness (new platform, need to build trust).
- Agency partnership network (needs active cultivation).

## 7. Technical Considerations

### Technical Requirements

**Platform:** Next.js (App Router), Tailwind CSS, shadcn/ui, SQLite via Prisma, JWT auth

**Integrations Required:**
- PPC Companion — shared authentication or SSO for cross-platform access.
- Email — for course notifications and certificate delivery.
- Payment gateway — for course enrollment.

**Technical Constraints:**
- No budget for AI/ML services — interactive tools must be logic-based.
- Must reuse UI components from PPC Companion where feasible (design system alignment).
- Lightweight deployment — Vercel compatible.

### Scale Requirements

**Expected Usage:**
- Users: 100–500 enrolled students in first year
- Concurrent live class participants: 20–50
- Interactive tool sessions: 50–200 daily

**Performance Requirements:**
- Page load < 2 seconds
- Tool interactions feel instant (< 500ms response)
- Certificates generate within 10 seconds

### Security & Compliance

**Security Requirements:**
- JWT-based authentication with HttpOnly cookies (same as PPC Companion)
- User data isolation per student
- Secure certificate generation

**Compliance Requirements:**
- Data Privacy Act (Philippines) compliance
- Standard web security best practices

## 8. Risks & Mitigation

### High Priority Risks

**Risk 1: Project Momentum Fails**
- Probability: High
- Impact: Critical
- Mitigation: Ship MVP fast with core curriculum + 1 interactive tool. Add features iteratively based on enrollment. Weekly progress check-ins.
- Owner: Ryan

**Risk 2: Low Enrollment**
- Probability: Medium
- Impact: High
- Mitigation: Leverage existing PPC Companion user base and ProjectAmazonPH Facebook community (15k+ followers) for launch.
- Owner: Ryan

**Risk 3: Tool Duplication with PPC Companion**
- Probability: Medium
- Impact: Medium
- Mitigation: Reuse components cleanly. Position Adcraft as advanced track, PPC Companion as foundations. Ensure complementary — not competing.
- Owner: Ryan

### Medium Priority Risks

- Students don't complete courses (mitigation: gamification + live classes create accountability)
- Agency partners don't materialize (mitigation: start with existing industry connections)
- Technical debt from rapid MVP (mitigation: Prisma schema design and component reuse)

### Assumptions

**Critical Assumptions:**
- PPC Companion graduates are willing to pay for advanced training.
- Agencies are actively hiring trained PPC specialists.
- Interactive tools provide enough value without AI-powered features.

**Validation Plan:**
1. Pre-launch survey to PPC Companion base — gauge interest and willingness to pay.
2. Pilot cohort (10–20 students) to validate curriculum and tools before full launch.
3. Agency outreach during development phase to confirm hiring demand.
